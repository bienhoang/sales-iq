## ICP 1: The Growth-Stage Founder

**Persona name**: Alex, Founder/CEO
**Company size**: 10-50 employees, Series A or bootstrapped
**Industry**: B2B SaaS

### Demographics & Psychographics
- Age: 30-45
- Highly technical or product background
- Data-driven decision maker
- Time-starved; values tools that save hours not minutes
- Reads newsletters over blogs; lurks on X/Twitter

### Pain Points
1. Marketing team is 1-2 people doing the work of 10
2. Inconsistent brand voice across channels as team grows
3. No system for content production — everything is ad-hoc
4. Hard to measure which marketing activities drive pipeline
5. Burning budget on agencies with slow turnaround

### Goals
- Hit next ARR milestone ($1M → $3M → $10M)
- Build a repeatable marketing engine before the next hire
- Establish category presence and brand recognition

### Preferred Channels
- LinkedIn (professional reading), X/Twitter (real-time), email newsletters, podcasts

### Objections
- "We don't have bandwidth to set this up"
- "Another tool to learn and maintain"
- "Will this actually work for our niche?"

### Trigger Events
- Just raised a round and needs to scale marketing
- Lost a deal because competitor had better brand presence
- New hire joining and needs systems in place

## ICP 2: The Scaling Marketing Lead

**Persona name**: Jordan, Head of Marketing
**Company size**: 50-200 employees, Series A/B
**Industry**: B2B SaaS or tech-enabled services

### Demographics & Psychographics
- Age: 28-40
- Generalist marketer managing a small team
- Accountable to pipeline and MQL targets
- Constantly context-switching between strategy and execution
- Active on LinkedIn; attends industry events

### Pain Points
1. Content production bottleneck — ideas exist but execution is slow
2. Difficult to maintain brand consistency with freelancers and contractors
3. Reporting across channels is manual and time-consuming
4. Campaign planning is reactive, not proactive
5. No single source of truth for brand assets and messaging

### Goals
- Build a content calendar that actually gets executed
- Prove marketing's contribution to revenue (attribution)
- Scale content output without proportional headcount growth

### Preferred Channels
- LinkedIn, email, industry Slack communities, G2/Capterra reviews

### Objections
- "Our brand is too complex for a templated approach"
- "We have an agency for this already"
- "I need my team to use it consistently, not just me"

### Trigger Events
- Board pressure to show marketing ROI
- Agency contract ending / looking for alternatives
- New product launch requiring multi-channel coordination

## ICP 3: The Solo Operator

**Persona name**: Sam, Founder-Marketer
**Company size**: 1-10 employees, pre-seed or bootstrapped
**Industry**: Any vertical SaaS or indie product

### Demographics & Psychographics
- Age: 25-40
- Does everything: product, support, marketing, sales
- Budget-conscious; high sensitivity to price/value
- Values speed and simplicity above all else
- Active in indie hacker / builder communities

### Pain Points
1. No time for marketing — always in reactive mode
2. Inconsistent posting schedule hurts organic growth
3. Writing copy is hard; not a natural writer
4. Doesn't know which channels to prioritize
5. Can't afford a marketing hire yet

### Goals
- Get to $10K MRR through organic and content marketing
- Build an audience that compounds over time
- Create marketing systems that run with minimal daily effort

### Preferred Channels
- X/Twitter, Reddit, indie hacker communities, Product Hunt, email

### Objections
- "I don't have time to set this up"
- "My audience is very niche, generic templates won't work"

### Trigger Events
- Just launched a product and needs distribution
- Plateau in organic growth after initial launch burst
- Seeing competitors gain traction through content
